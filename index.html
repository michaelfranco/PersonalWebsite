GoodWorld Virtual Wallet

Project Management | Stakeholder Relations | User Research

2.5 Weeks

Team of 3

The Story

GoodWorld ​​is a fundraising start­up that makes giving to great causes instant with a hashtag. Use '#donate' in a tweet or Facebook post to support causes like UNICEF. GoodWorld has been very successful and is now looking for a way to integrate the ability to gift and receive donation credits, manage donor matching, and incentivize giving.

The Challenge

GoodWorld would like to implement a wallet experience into their product line.

MY ROLE

From the initial kick-off, our team assembled to determine how we’d best work with each other. We expressed our strengths, weaknesses, and goals toward the project. Design is first. Then naps and food. We made sure that we were working toward the design, not toward our own self-interests. With that in mind, from the previous project (you can read about this here), I knew I had to take the reins to prevent 1)analysis paralysis and 2)keep the team moving toward design goals. I asked from the get-go if anyone wanted to take the lead, and so I stepped up. As a result, my role throughout the project was a project manager, keeping the team in line with our timeline and goals (insert timeline here). If not with the team, I was the main point of contact whenever communication with shareholders was necessary. Thus, it was my responsibility to mange expectations of shareholders. I also created unique contextual interviews to determine user behavior, aside from the initial testing. I was also trying to make sure that were drawing appropriate conclusions from our research.

WHAT IS A VIRTUAL WALLET?

A wallet in its physical form contains many things. They could include money, photos, reward cards, etc. So in the case of a virtual wallet, what would that entail? This is the question we had to ask ourselves.
